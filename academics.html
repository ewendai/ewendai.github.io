Summer 2018

  • Math 128A: Numerical Analysis
In Math 128A, we did 2 fairly small projects that were basically coding what we learned in class in Matlab. Looking back, I should have enrolled in more than one class, or signed up for more, as I had much more free time than I knew what to do . . . so I learned some life skills, like cooking. Not that I'm any good at it, but I would say that I can survive. Over the summer, I considered what type of career I would enjoy in the future and the type of skills relevant to it. I decided that I am interested in working towards being a Data Scientist, as it allows me to combine my interest in working with applied mathematics while still utilizing the unendingly helpful tool that is the computer. Therefore, I decided to, once again, change my majors to Data Science and Statistics, as I felt that these two majors very nicely compliment each other.

Spring 2018

  • CompSci 61B: Data Structures
  • CompSci 70: Discrete Mathematics and Probability Theory
  • Music 25: Introduction to Music Theory
I really enjoyed CompSci 70 and the beauty of logic and mathematics: everything just fits together so nicely and so perfectly. While taking CompSci 61B, however, I realized that I did not want to pursue a Computer Science major. While coding and completing projects are fun (sometimes!), they do not grant me the same satisfaction that working with numbers does. This is why at this point in my academic career I've decided to switch to doubling in Data Science and Applied Mathematics. Additionally, this semester I took a lesson on biting off more than I can chew in stretching myself too thin and trying to get involved in everything I am interested.
